# Copywriting Guide — White Hart Hotel, Holsworthy
## Complete Briefing Document — Pub/Hotel Niche, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Most pub and hotel websites read identically: "A warm welcome awaits you at [name], nestled in the heart of [place]." This generic copy is invisible — it says nothing, builds no trust, and converts no one. Outstanding hospitality copy does the opposite: it tells a specific story, speaks directly to the visitor's desires, and makes them feel they're already there. This guide covers every aspect of copywriting needed for White Hart Hotel.

---

## 1. Brand Voice Definition

Before writing a single word, define how White Hart sounds. A clear voice makes all copy decisions easier and ensures consistency across the website, social media, emails, and printed materials.

### Recommended Voice for White Hart Hotel

**Personality traits:**
- **Warm** — like a greeting from a landlord who knows your name
- **Genuine** — no corporate polish, no hollow phrases
- **Proud** — of Devon, of the food, of the community
- **Unpretentious** — good quality without snobbery
- **Local** — rooted in Holsworthy, the agricultural calendar, the landscape

**Voice in practice:**

| DON'T say | DO say |
|----------|-------|
| "We pride ourselves on delivering exceptional hospitality" | "We just try to be the best pub in town. Most days, we reckon we are." |
| "A warm welcome awaits" | "Pull up a chair. You're among friends." |
| "Situated in the charming market town of Holsworthy" | "We're right in the middle of Holsworthy — proper Devon country town, market every Tuesday." |
| "Our team of experienced chefs" | "Our kitchen team cooks with ingredients that were growing nearby last week." |
| "Enjoy a delightful dining experience" | "Come hungry. Leave happy." |
| "Well-appointed en-suite bedrooms" | "Comfortable rooms with everything you need and nothing you don't." |

## 3. Room Description Copywriting

### The Formula: Feel > Features > Facts

Bad room description:
> "Room 3 is a double en-suite room with TV, tea and coffee facilities, and free WiFi."

Good room description:
> **The Garden Room**
> Tucked at the back of the hotel, the Garden Room overlooks the courtyard and catches the morning light something beautiful. It's the quiet one — ideal if you want a proper night's sleep after a day on the moors. King-sized bed, walk-in shower, and the best views of any room we have.
> **Sleeps:** 2 | **Bed:** King | **Bathroom:** En-suite with walk-in shower
> *From £X per night including breakfast*
> [Book This Room]

### Room Copy Principles
1. Give each room a name, not a number
2. Lead with the feeling/experience, not the inventory
3. Use sensory language: morning light, quiet, warm, spacious
4. Mention the best thing about the room early
5. End with practical details (bed size, bathroom, wifi)
6. Always include price from and a direct booking CTA
7. Suggest what this room is "ideal for": couples, business travellers, dog owners

### Room Copy Vocabulary Bank

**Evocative adjectives:** Snug, airy, peaceful, characterful, sunlit, cosy, generous, well-considered

**Sensory language:** "The sound of the market below on Tuesday mornings", "morning light through the casement windows", "the smell of breakfast from downstairs", "thick curtains that keep the Devon darkness properly dark"

**Differentiation phrases:** 
- "Our most popular room" (social proof)
- "Best for couples wanting a proper retreat"
- "The one guests always request on their second visit"
- "Faces west — the sunsets are something else"

## 4. Food & Menu Copywriting

### Menu Language Psychology

Research shows that descriptive menu language increases sales by 27% and perceived quality by 35%.

**Bad menu copy:**
> Beef burger with chips — £13.50

**Good menu copy:**
> **Devon Beef Burger**
> Locally-sourced Devon beef, aged cheddar, caramelised onions, smoked bacon, brioche bun, proper chips or sweet potato fries
> £13.50

**Excellent menu copy:**
> **White Hart Burger**
> Our beef comes from [Farm Name] just up the road near Holsworthy — hung for 21 days and minced fresh for us twice a week. Topped with West Country cheddar, homemade chutney and lettuce from [local supplier]. Best burger in North Devon. (We're biased, obviously.)
> £14.50

### Provenance Copywriting
Name your suppliers wherever possible. This is not showing off — it builds trust and tells a story.

Examples:
- "Aged beef from [local farm], sourced within 10 miles"
- "Cheese from [local cheesemaker], made [X] miles away"
- "Vegetables from [local market garden]"
- "Cream and butter from a Devon dairy"
- "Lamb reared on Dartmoor"

### Menu Page Introduction Copy

> We cook what's good right now. The menu changes with the seasons — sometimes it changes because something brilliant turned up from a local farm that morning. We try to source as much as possible from Devon and the surrounding counties, not because it's trendy, but because the ingredients genuinely taste better.
> If you have allergies or dietary requirements, please tell us. We can accommodate most things with advance notice.

### Specials Board Copy (for website specials section)
> **This Week's Specials**
> [Date range]
> Our kitchen team writes the specials based on what's looking great at the market and what's come in from local suppliers this week. These change frequently — check back or follow us on Facebook to see what's on the board.

## 5. Events & Functions Copywriting

### Private Hire Page

**Hero heading:** 
> We love hosting. Tell us what you're planning.

**Body copy:**
> Whether it's a milestone birthday, a wake that celebrates a life well lived, a business away-day, or just too many people to fit at yours — the White Hart has room for it.
>
> Our [function room] holds up to [X] people for a sit-down dinner, [Y] standing. We can do full catering or just bar and nibbles. We've hosted everything from wedding receptions to baby showers to surprise 70ths to actual surprise parties where the guest of honour genuinely didn't know.
>
> Get in touch and tell us what you have in mind. We're flexible, we're local, and we know how to make a night out of it.
>
> [Enquire About Private Hire] [Download Our Functions Pack]

### Weddings Page

**Opening:**
> **Your wedding at the White Hart, Holsworthy**
>
> We're not a wedding factory. We don't host three weddings a weekend and move you along like a production line. When we take on a wedding, we give it everything.
>
> Set in the heart of a proper Devon market town, the White Hart offers [civil ceremony licence/reception licence] for intimate to [X]-guest celebrations. Local caterers, local flowers, local soul.

**Key wedding page elements:**
- Capacity numbers (essential for first-visit qualification)
- Licence type (civil ceremony, reception only)
- Catering options (in-house or external)
- Accommodation for wedding party
- Coordinator contact details
- Real wedding photos (not stock images)
- Testimonials from previous couples
- Pricing guide or "packages from £X" (removes the "how much?" barrier to enquiry)

## 6. About Us / Our Story Page

This is the most underused high-value page on most hospitality websites. Done well, it builds trust and connection that converts browsers into loyal customers.

### Story Structure (StoryBrand Framework Applied)

**1. Who are you?** (Not your history first — your character)
**2. What do you care about?** (Values, not just facts)
**3. What do you offer?** (The result for the customer)
**4. Why this place?** (Connection to Holsworthy)

**Example "About Us" Draft:**

> **Our Story**
>
> The White Hart has been standing in the centre of Holsworthy longer than anyone can quite remember. The building has changed, the landlords have changed, the menus have changed — but the idea has stayed the same: a proper local pub where everyone's welcome.
>
> We took over [year / "a few years back"] because we love Holsworthy and we love what a good pub does for a community. A place where the farmers come in after market day. Where the kids get a Coke while mum and dad have a proper pint. Where someone celebrates their retirement and someone else holds a wake and both groups end up chatting at the bar.
>
> The food matters to us. We buy local because the stuff that's grown and reared nearby genuinely tastes better. We don't pretend to be a fine dining restaurant — we're a pub that cooks with care.
>
> The rooms matter to us too. If you're staying with us, you're trusting us with your sleep. We take that seriously.
>
> We're not perfect, but we're proud of what we do. Come and find out for yourself.
>
> [Owner name(s)] — White Hart Hotel, Holsworthy

## 7. Local Area Copy

A "Things to Do" or "Exploring Devon" page serves dual purposes: it adds genuine value for visitors AND it captures SEO traffic for people researching the area.

### Page Introduction:
> **Exploring from the White Hart**
>
> Holsworthy is one of those proper Devon towns that doesn't try to be touristy and is all the better for it. It's a working market town — Tuesdays are market day and it's been that way for centuries. From here, you're 20 minutes from the coast at Bude, half an hour from Dartmoor, and right in the middle of some of the best walking and cycling in the South West.
>
> Here's what we'd recommend if you're staying with us:

### Activity Sections to Include:
- Walking: Tarka Trail, SWCP coastal section from Bude, Dartmoor day trips
- Cycling: Rail Trail, local routes
- Coast: Bude, Widemouth Bay, Crackington Haven (40-60 min drive)
- Dartmoor: Okehampton, Belstone, Yes Tor
- Market day: Holsworthy Tuesday pannier market
- Holsworthy Agricultural Show (major annual event — July)
- Fishing: Rivers Tamar and Torridge
- Golf: Local courses

## 8. Call-to-Action Copy

CTAs are the most tested element on hospitality websites. Specific and low-friction beats generic every time.

### CTA Copy for Accommodation

❌ "Book Now" (generic, no commitment reduction)  
✅ "Check Room Availability" (lower commitment, specific)  
✅ "Book Direct — Best Rate Guaranteed" (adds incentive)  
✅ "See What's Available This Weekend" (time-specific, lower pressure)

### CTA Copy for Food

❌ "Book a Table" (fine but generic)  
✅ "Reserve a Table for Tonight" (time-specific)  
✅ "Book Sunday Lunch" (specific occasion)  
✅ "See Today's Menu & Reserve" (reduces uncertainty before commitment)

### CTA Copy for Events

❌ "Contact Us" (vague)  
✅ "Tell Us About Your Event" (inviting, action-specific)  
✅ "Enquire About Private Hire" (specific)  
✅ "Start Planning Your Wedding" (aspirational, specific stage)

## 9. Review Response Copy

Responding to reviews IS copy — it's public, searchable, and shapes brand perception.

### Formula for Positive Review Response:
> [Personalise with specific detail from their review] + [Thank them genuinely] + [Reinforce something positive about the business] + [Invite them back with a specific hook]

**Example:**
> "So glad the Sunday lamb was a hit, [Name] — it came from [local farm] and we're really proud of what our kitchen team does with it. Lovely to hear you're planning to bring the family next time too. We'll get a table ready with your name on it! See you soon."

### Formula for Negative Review Response:
> [Acknowledge specific concern] + [Apologise genuinely without excuses] + [Brief context where relevant] + [Invite to resolve offline]

**Example:**
> "We're really sorry to read this, [Name] — this isn't the experience we want anyone to have at the White Hart. [Specific acknowledgment of their issue]. Please do get in touch with us directly on [phone/email] and we'd genuinely like to make it right. Thank you for taking the time to let us know."

## 12. Copy Mistakes to Avoid — The Hospitality Cliché Blacklist

Cut these phrases from every piece of copy:

- "A warm welcome awaits" ❌
- "We pride ourselves on..." ❌
- "Nestled in the heart of..." ❌
- "Second to none" ❌
- "Exceptional" (unless earned and specific) ❌
- "State of the art facilities" ❌
- "Well-appointed" ❌
- "A delightful experience" ❌
- "Our passion for..." ❌
- "We are delighted to..." ❌
- "The perfect venue for all occasions" ❌
- "Something for everyone" ❌
- "We look forward to welcoming you" ❌ (end with something more specific and genuine)

**The test:** Could another pub in England use this sentence? If yes, rewrite it until only White Hart could say it.

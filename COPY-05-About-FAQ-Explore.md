---

### SECTION: THE COAST

**H2:** The Coast — 20 minutes away

> Bude is the closest town on the coast — about 17 miles west. It's a proper surf town: a clean beach, a good tidal swimming pool cut into the rocks, and some excellent places to eat and drink. Widemouth Bay is 3 miles south of Bude and possibly the better beach — long, sandy, and less crowded in summer.
>
> If you want cliffs and drama, Crackington Haven is about 25 miles away — one of the most spectacular stretches of the South West Coast Path, with 400-foot cliffs and very little in the way of tourist infrastructure. You'll need sturdy shoes and a packed lunch.
>
> **From the White Hart to Bude:** 25 minutes by car. No public transport worth mentioning.

**Distance quick-reference:**
- Bude: 17 miles · ~25 min
- Widemouth Bay: 20 miles · ~28 min
- Crackington Haven: 24 miles · ~40 min
- Boscastle: 28 miles · ~45 min
- Tintagel: 31 miles · ~50 min

---

### SECTION: DARTMOOR

**H2:** Dartmoor — 30 minutes away

> Dartmoor's northern edge is about 15 miles east of Holsworthy — roughly half an hour by car. Okehampton is the closest town and makes a good base for the moor if you want to go further. Belstone is a beautiful moorland village with excellent walking right from the car park. Yes Tor and High Willhays (the highest point in southern England at 621m) are accessible from there in good weather.
>
> Dartmoor in good visibility is genuinely extraordinary — open moorland, ancient standing stones, wild ponies, and almost nobody about on a weekday in autumn. Go in good weather and go early.
>
> **Note:** Dartmoor is exposed upland terrain. Proper footwear, waterproofs, and a map are genuinely not optional.

**Dartmoor highlights from Holsworthy:**
- Okehampton: 15 miles · ~25 min
- Belstone: 17 miles · ~28 min
- Yes Tor: 18 miles · ~30 min
- Lydford Gorge (NT): 14 miles · ~22 min
- Castle Drogo (NT): 25 miles · ~40 min

### SECTION: HOLSWORTHY ITSELF

**H2:** Holsworthy — the town on your doorstep

> Don't sleep on Holsworthy itself. It's a proper working Devon market town with an independent high street, a traditional pannier market on Tuesdays (one of the longest-running in Devon), and the [St Peter's Fair in July — one of the oldest fairs in England / Holsworthy Agricultural Show — one of the largest one-day shows in the South West].
>
> **Holsworthy Pannier Market:** Every Tuesday from early morning. Local produce, crafts, and the kind of atmosphere you don't find in a supermarket.
>
> **St Peter's Fair:** [July] — [brief description if applicable]
>
> **Holsworthy Agricultural Show:** [July] — One of the most significant agricultural shows in the South West. The town fills up; book your room well in advance if you're coming for it.
>
> The church of [St Peter and St Paul] is worth a look — one of the finest in Devon. The town itself doesn't take long to explore on foot, but the surrounding country is the point.

---

### SECTION: FURTHER AFIELD

**H2:** A bit further — day trips worth the drive

> **Exmoor (50 miles):** Different character to Dartmoor — greener, gentler, with dramatic coastal cliffs at the Valley of the Rocks near Lynton. Worth a day if you have the time.
>
> **Padstow, Cornwall (40 miles):** Rick Stein, the Camel Estuary, the Camel Trail cycle route. A proper foodie destination on a good day. Can get very busy in summer.
>
> **Boscastle & Tintagel, Cornwall (30 miles):** Boscastle's harbour is spectacular, the Museum of Witchcraft and Magic is genuinely interesting, and Tintagel's Arthurian mythology is worth engaging with even if the crowds are a bit much.
>
> **Exeter (50 miles):** For a proper city day — the cathedral, good independent shopping, and one of the best high streets in the South West. About an hour's drive.

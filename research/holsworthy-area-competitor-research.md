## 1. HOLSWORTHY TOWN INFORMATION

### Basic Facts
- **Status:** Market town and civil parish in the Torridge district of Devon
- **Location:** 36 miles (58 km) west of Exeter
- **Population:** 2,641 (2011 census), estimated 3,287 (2019)
- **Area:** Lies in the heart of Ruby Country, north-west Devon
- **Heritage:** Market and fair charters believed to date from the 12th century

### Market Days
- **Wednesday Pannier Market:** Every Wednesday, 9am - 3pm in the Market Square
  - Stalls selling arts, crafts, fruit & vegetables, clothing, refreshments, local seasonal produce
- **Livestock Market:** One of the newest, busiest, and largest weekly livestock markets in the country
  - Cattle, sheep, calves, dairy, pigs, poultry
  - Monthly collective machinery sale (usually 1st Saturday of each month)

### Annual Events
1. **St Peter's Fair** (July) - Week-long festival
   - Charter dictates it falls on the first Wednesday after the first Friday in July
   - Starts with Civic Service on the first Sunday
   - Fair Wednesday: Charter reading, Pretty Maid reveals through west door of church at noon
   - Four nights of free live music in the square
   - Funfair, exhibitions, competitions, car boot sale, teddy bears picnic, junior town crier competition, furry dance, treasure hunt

2. **Pretty Maid Competition** - Part of St Peter's Fair
   - Founded mid-19th century by the brother of local vicar Rev. Thomas Meyrick
   - Annual bequest to a young single woman under 30 who is "handsome, deserving and devout"
   - Identity kept secret until she emerges through the church belfry door at noon on Fair Day
   - She then opens the funfair

3. **Holsworthy Carnival** (November)
   - Began November 1900 to replace Guy Fawkes/bonfire celebrations
   - Devon's last carnival of the season
   - Attracts many floats and large crowds

4. **Holsworthy & Stratton Agricultural Show** (3rd Thursday in August)
   - Began in 1883
   - Held at Killatree Cross, 1.2 miles west of Holsworthy
   - Stock, horses, and ponies are the main feature
   - Full timetable of activities and entertainment

5. **Holsworthy Vintage Vehicle and Engine Rally** (last Saturday & Sunday of June)
   - Dates from 1987
   - Two-day event attracting exhibitors and visitors from wide area

6. **Ruby Run** (June)
   - Half-marathon between Hatherleigh and Holsworthy

### Transport Links
- **Nearest Train Station:** Okehampton (reopened as the Dartmoor Line)
  - Hourly trains on the Dartmoor Line
  - Bus connections available from Okehampton to Holsworthy, Bude, and Launceston
  - New Okehampton Interchange under construction to improve bus-rail integration
- **Historical:** Holsworthy had its own railway station (1879-1966, closed by Beeching Axe)
- **A-Roads:** A3072 (east-west, towards Bideford/Crediton), A388 (north-south, towards Launceston/Bude)
- **Driving Times:** Exeter ~1hr, Bude ~20 mins, Plymouth ~1hr

## 4. LOCAL ATTRACTIONS (For Website Content)

### Bude & Beaches - 9 miles / ~20 minutes drive
- **Summerleaze Beach:** Less than 5 min walk from Bude centre. Large sea pool at foot of cliffs (opened 1930). Sandy beach, popular with families
- **Crooklets Beach:** Popular with surfers, home to Bude Surf Life Saving Club. Wide golden sand at low tide, rocky outcrops for rock-pooling
- **Widemouth Bay:** 3 miles south of Bude. Perfect for learning to surf/bodyboard. Wide sandy beach
- **Other activities in Bude:** Golf course, Bude Canal (walking, kayaking), South West Coast Path, good restaurants and cafes
- **Closest beach to Holsworthy:** Summerleaze Beach at 8.65 miles

### RHS Garden Rosemoor - 16 miles / ~25-30 minutes drive
- **Location:** 1 mile south of Great Torrington on A3124
- **Opening Times:**
  - April-September: 10am-6pm (last entry 5pm)
  - October-March: 10am-5pm (last entry 4pm)
- **Description:** 65 acres of spectacular garden in the Torridge Valley. One of four RHS gardens in England
- **Features:** Rose gardens, cottage garden, fruit & vegetable garden, arboretum, woodland walks, seasonal flower displays
- **Getting there from Holsworthy:** ~25 min drive via A3072/B3227, or bus 85 to Barnstaple then 5B

### Dartmoor National Park - 37 miles by road / ~50 minutes to edge
- **Size:** 368 square miles of open moorland, river valleys, and ancient woodland
- **Key features:** Wild ponies, granite tors, Bronze Age settlements, Wistman's Wood
- **Activities:** Walking, hiking, rock climbing, letterboxing/geocaching, wild swimming, horse riding
- **Key towns:** Okehampton (nearest to Holsworthy), Tavistock, Princetown, Moretonhampstead
- **Note:** The northern edge of Dartmoor (Okehampton area) is closer - roughly 25 miles

### Roadford Lake - ~10 miles / ~25 minutes drive
- **Location:** Wolf Valley, between Okehampton and Launceston, on Devon-Cornwall border
- **Description:** Spectacular lake set on the edge of Dartmoor
- **Activities:** Kayaking, paddleboarding, sailing, fishing, climbing, archery, den building, woodland exploration, mini-beast hunts, cycling, camping
- **Great for:** Families with children, outdoor adventure enthusiasts
- **Facilities:** Activity centre, cafe, trails

### Tamar Valley - ~15 miles south
- **Status:** National Landscape (formerly AONB), straddling Devon-Cornwall border
- **Key features:**
  - Tamar Valley Discovery Trail - 31 miles through the heart of the valley
  - Scenic Tamar Valley railway line
  - Cotehele - National Trust medieval house with gardens
  - Buckland Abbey - 700-year-old National Trust property
  - Morwellham Quay - award-winning Cornish Mining World Heritage Site
  - Kit Hill - 334m, highest point with 360-degree views
- **Activities:** Walking, kayaking, fishing, cycling, mining heritage, boat trips from Plymouth
- **Key towns:** Launceston, Tavistock, Callington, Saltash

### Launceston Castle - 14 miles / ~20 minutes drive
- **Managed by:** English Heritage
- **Description:** Norman castle with a commanding hilltop position above the town
- **Features:** Shell keep, panoramic views of Dartmoor and Bodmin Moor
- **Town of Launceston also offers:** Steam railway, independent shops, South Gate medieval arch
- **Just across the border in Cornwall**

### Ruby Country
- **What it is:** The name for the rural inland hinterland of north-west Devon, covering 45 parishes around Holsworthy and Hatherleigh
- **Named after:** Hardy Devon Red cattle called "Rubies" which thrive on the local grazing
- **Size:** 70,467 hectares (twice the size of the Isle of Wight)
- **Population:** ~18,000 inhabitants
- **Origin:** Established as a not-for-profit partnership after the 2001 Foot and Mouth Disease outbreak to promote tourism and sustainability
- **Activities:** Walking, cycling, horse riding, carriage driving, fishing
- **Character:** One of the most unspoilt, rural areas of Devon. Rolling farmland, narrow lanes, hidden valleys

### The Tarka Trail
- **Total length:** 180 miles of walking and cycling trail through North Devon and Exmoor
- **Traffic-free section:** 30 miles between Braunton and Meeth - the longest traffic-free cycle route in the UK
- **Surface:** Flat, tarmacked, former railway line (ideal for families and beginners)
- **Named after:** Henry Williamson's novel "Tarka the Otter"
- **Route:** North loop through Exmoor; South loop follows River Torridge through Torrington, turning at Okehampton
- **Key access points:** Braunton, Barnstaple, Fremington Quay, Instow, Bideford, Torrington, Meeth
- **Facilities:** Cycle hire shops at all major access points (including electric bikes)
- **Nearest access from Holsworthy:** Meeth or Great Torrington sections (~16 miles)

### Holsworthy Market
- **Day:** Every Wednesday, 9am-3pm
- **Location:** Market Square (Pannier Market)
- **What's sold:**
  - Local seasonal produce, fruit & vegetables
  - Arts, crafts, and supplies
  - Clothing
  - Refreshments and hot food
- **Livestock Market (separate):** Cattle, sheep, calves, dairy, pigs, poultry
- **Heritage:** Charter dating from the 12th century
- **Character:** Farmers from far and wide converge; locals mingle with visitors
